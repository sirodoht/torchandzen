Torch and Zen

Spend 4 days building an AI product while being Zen.
What?

Join a bunch of people. Learn by doing. Explore new technologies. Focus without distractions. Work on something exciting.

Join us

Submit your Excitement Plan »

To take part, you just need to figure out what you want to work on to stay excited while being there! You will have 4 days. Treat them as your time budget which you spend on thinking and writing fun code!

FAQ

Torch and Zen will take place
15 – 18 September 2023
in Greenwich, London

What do I need to join?

  1. Figure out what you want to work on to stay excited during this event.
  2. Answer our questions in the Excitement Plan!
  3. Done!

How much does it cost?

£70 per person.

Do I work alone or in teams?

Teams are preferred! Invite your friends or make new friends at the event :D

Where does it take place?

We are fortunate enough to have access to the beautiful office of Founders and Coders! Located at the Design District of North Greenwich, London, England.

Who is this for?

This event is aimed at people who are comfortable coding on their own and want to explore the new space of AI.

Where is the Zen?

  1. Meditation every morning
  2. Lunch all together
  3. Daily check-ins with fellow attendees
  4. Daily check-outs and self-reflections
  5. Focus on one thing
  6. No distractions

When is it again?

Starts at 9am on Friday 15 September 2023 and finishes at 5pm on Monday 18 September. 4 full days of fun.

Is this a hackathon?

Kind of!

Are there any prizes?

No.

What happens in the end?

During Day 4, the last day, we present our work to the fellow attendees and our invited friends. Presentation is optional.

Why does it cost that?

We want to cover food plus some other comforts. See our budget here.

Who’s organising this?

See here.

What’s with the name?

PyTorch is the most popular deep learning library. Zen is a buddhist word that has come to mean deep calmness and inner peace.

Why are you doing this?

We think it would be fun if there was a space where people can do programming without the oppression of having to be the best or to change the world. We still value excellence and usefulness highly — but we also reserve space for empathy and kindness.

Should I join?

Do you think you will have fun participating? Do you think you will enjoy spending time here? If so, then please join!
